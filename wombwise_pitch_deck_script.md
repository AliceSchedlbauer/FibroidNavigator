# WombWise Pitch Deck Script

## 60-Second Opening

In the first 15 seconds:

"When I was dealing with fibroid concerns myself, the hardest part was not one appointment. It was the daily uncertainty: Is this bleeding normal? Is my fatigue from stress or iron loss? What should I do today while I wait for care? WombWise turns that uncertainty into a 30-second daily check-in and a clear next step."

In the first 30 seconds:

"The wedge is simple: start with women who already suspect fibroids or have risk factors and need daily guidance before the system gives them a clear diagnosis or treatment path. The demand is visible in the current behavior: women search forums, screenshots of lab reports, diet lists, cycle notes, and appointment availability because no single trusted product connects symptoms, cycle timing, blood markers, lifestyle guidance, and specialist booking."

Core pitch:

"WombWise is a cycle-based fibroid prevention and care navigation product. A user enters when her bleeding started, when the previous bleeding started, stress level, food notes, symptoms, and optional lab values from a medical report. WombWise calculates her personal cycle length, flags relevant symptom and blood-marker patterns, gives three practical micro-actions for the day, and, when risk is elevated, shows a Doctolib-ready path to a gynecology specialist."

"The status quo is fragmented. Cycle apps track periods but do not understand fibroid risk. Clinic pages explain treatments but do not help day by day. Wellness apps give generic diet advice. Appointment tools help only after a user already knows what to book. WombWise starts earlier and connects the daily pattern to the care pathway."

"The buyer is a health-conscious woman aged 25 to 50 with heavy bleeding, pelvic pressure, family history, anemia concerns, or known fibroids. The first paid channel can be direct-to-consumer, then expand into gynecology clinics, fertility clinics, employer women's health benefits, and digital health partners that need better triage and retention."

"Why now: fibroids affect a large share of women during reproductive years, with disproportionate burden among Black women. More women now have access to lab reports, cycle data, and telehealth booking, but that information is still not translated into an everyday care plan. WombWise is built for that shift."

"My non-copyable evidence is founder-market fit. I have lived the gap WombWise solves: the waiting, the uncertainty, and the need to translate symptoms and reports into action. That lived experience shapes what the product asks, what it avoids, and why it focuses on report-confirmed inputs instead of guessing."

"The product is already demo-ready: personalized cycle tracking, blood-marker insights, daily micro-actions, an 87% high-risk demo case, bleeding visualization, and a booking flow for gynecology specialists. The wedge is not replacing doctors. The wedge is becoming the trusted daily companion before and between visits."

## Slide-By-Slide Script

### 1. Problem

"Millions of women live with heavy bleeding, fatigue, pelvic pressure, and uncertainty before they get clear fibroid care. The daily question is not only 'Do I have fibroids?' It is 'What should I do today, and when should I escalate?'"

### 2. Status Quo

"Today, the journey is split across cycle trackers, symptom notes, lab PDFs, generic diet content, and appointment search. Women carry the burden of connecting all of it themselves."

### 3. Wedge

"WombWise starts with the daily check-in: cycle timing, stress, food, symptoms, and optional lab values. That is the wedge because it is frequent, personal, and happens before treatment decisions."

### 4. Product

"The product calculates individual cycle length from real period dates, separates report-confirmed clinical inputs from daily wellness inputs, identifies risk patterns, and returns three actions the user can take today. If risk is elevated, it moves directly into specialist booking readiness."

### 5. Buyer

"The first buyer is a woman aged 25 to 50 who has heavy bleeding, anemia concerns, fibroid family history, or known fibroids and wants guidance between appointments. The expansion buyers are gynecology clinics, fertility clinics, and women's health benefits."

### 6. Demand

"Demand already exists in the workaround. Women are logging symptoms manually, asking online communities to interpret bleeding and lab values, searching diet advice, and hunting for specialists. WombWise turns that scattered behavior into one guided flow."

### 7. Why Now

"Women have more access to their own health data than ever: cycle records, lab portals, imaging reports, and online booking. But access alone is not care. WombWise converts that data into an understandable next step."

### 8. Founder-Market Fit

"I am building this because I have experienced the problem directly. That matters here because trust, wording, and timing are the product. WombWise asks for documented medical inputs where needed and gives daily guidance without pretending to replace a clinician."

### 9. Evidence

"The current demo already shows the core loop: a 30-second check-in, personalized cycle calculation, symptom and blood-marker assessment, bleeding chart, 87% risk demo case, and a gynecology booking path. The evidence is not a concept slide; it is an end-to-end working flow."

### 10. Close

"WombWise helps women act earlier, ask better questions, and reach care faster. The vision is simple: every woman at fibroid risk should know what is happening in her body today and what step to take next."
